\section{Problem}
( 4.12) Determine P(E$\mid$F),if a die is thrown three times,\\
E : 4 appears on third toss\\ F : 6 and 5 appears on respectively on first and second toss.

\section{Solution}
Sample space = 6*6*6 = 216 \\ \\ Then the event E of getting 4 at third throw of die would be =
$\{ (1,1,4),(2,1,4)............(6,1,4)\\(1,2,4),(2,2,4)............(6,2,4)\\(1,3,4),(2,3,4)............(6,3,4)\\(1,4,4),(2,4,4)............(6,4,4)\\(1,5,4),(2,5,4)............(6,5,4)\\(1,6,4),(2,6,4)............(6,6,4) \}$\\ \\probability of event E , Pr(E) = $\frac{36}{216} $ 
                              = $\frac{1}{6}$\\
                              
Event F , getting 6 and 5 on first and second throw respectively = $\{(6,5,1),(6,5,2),(6,5,3)\\,(6,5,4),(6,5,5),(6,5,6)\}$ \\ \\therefore ,\\ Probability of event F  , Pr(F) = $\frac{6}{216}$ =  $\frac{1}{36}$  \\ \\ E$\cap $F = $\{(6,5,4)\}$ \\ Probability of E$\cap $F i.e getting 6,5 and 4 on first ,second and third throw respectively, Pr(E$\cap$F) = $\frac{1}{216}$ \\ \\ Probability P(E$\mid$F) = $\frac{P(E\cap F)}{P(F)}$ 
$= \frac{1/216}{6/216}$
$ = \frac{1}{6}$
